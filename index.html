🏆 Today's BTS Pick — Tue Mar 31
LUIS ARRAEZ
2B · San Francisco Giants · vs. Germán Márquez (RHP) · Petco Park · ~9:40 PM ET
The choice here comes down to pitcher quality. Germán Márquez had the highest ERA in baseball among qualified starters in 2025 at 6.70, allowed a .393 wOBA, and gave up 91.7 mph average exit velocity against — numbers that put him in the bottom 3% of the league. He signed a minor-league deal with San Diego this winter and has worked his way into the rotation, but none of the underlying issues have resolved. His velocity is still below-career average post-Tommy John and his command of his secondary pitches is inconsistent. Arraez, meanwhile, is in the middle of a brutal BABIP correction to start 2026 (.202 actual wOBA vs .351 xwOBA) — he is making contact at his normal elite rate, the balls just aren't dropping. That correction is due. Arraez vs Márquez is the most extreme mismatch of elite contact hitter vs elite vulnerability pitcher on today's slate. Yes, Petco Park suppresses offense — but no park suppresses a hitter who simply refuses to strike out. Arraez's 3.1% K-rate and 5.3% whiff rate make him immune to the pitcher-park discount that hurts power hitters.
